Beaches in Kanyakumari
  • Kanyakumari

    Kanyakumari Beach holds an esteemed position in every sightseeing tour, captivating numerous visitors who gather here to witness the enchanting sunsets and sunrises. Nestled amidst rugged cliffs, this stunning location offers a spectacle of crashing waves. While it ranks among Kanyakumari's finest beaches and attracts a multitude of visitors daily, swimming and surfing should be approached with caution due to the rough sea conditions. Instead, take in the mesmerizing waves and patiently await the glorious sunset that paints the horizon with vibrant hues. What sets Kanyakumari Beach apart is its convergence of three majestic bodies of waterâ€”the Bay of Bengal, the Arabian Sea, and the Indian Ocean. This unique feature provides a haven for painters and photographers to unleash their artistic talents with ease. For a breathtaking view of the sea and sky, ascend the lighthouse and indulge in the scenic panorama that unfolds before you.

  • Sanguthurai

    Sanguthurai Beach, in contrast to Kanyakumari Beach, exudes a serene ambiance. This tranquil oasis offers a plethora of opportunities for relaxation, accompanied by amenities such as a children's play area and food booths. Located approximately 9 km from Nagercoil town, the beach holds historical significance with a remarkable conch monument dating back to the Chola period. The pristine white sands of this coastal gem serve as a picturesque backdrop to the shoreline. Additionally, from Sanguthurai Beach, one can catch a glimpse of the majestic Vivekananda Rock Memorial. Keep a keen eye, and you may even spot a striking white pillar emerging from the depths of the sea at Sanguthurai Beach.

  • Sothavilai

    Sothavilai Beach, ranking among the top attractions in Kanyakumari, proudly stands as one of the longest stretches of coastline in the region. Located in close proximity to Sanguthurai Beach and approximately 10 km away from Kanyakumari, this picturesque beach spans about four kilometers, offering ample spaces for relaxation and relishing the refreshing sea breeze. Despite being affected by the devastating tsunami in 2004, Sothavilai Beach has undergone extensive renovations and now emanates a warm and inviting atmosphere. While the beach may lack certain amenities, it compensates with tranquility, making it an ideal destination for those seeking peace. Additionally, a small tower stands nearby, providing a perfect vantage point to soak in the breathtaking scenery

  • Muttom

    Muttom Beach, located in Kanyakumari, stands as a renowned coastal destination celebrated for its striking natural allure. The beach presents a pristine and captivating panorama, adorned with imposing boulders that gracefully interrupt the crashing waves. Venture atop these rugged formations to indulge in a truly breathtaking sight of the expansive sea.

  • Thengapattinam

    Thengapattinam Beach, adorned with verdant coconut palms, boasts exquisite beauty that entices visitors. However, keep in mind that reaching Thengapattinam Beach requires a substantial drive from Kanyakumari, spanning approximately 50 kilometers. Rest assured, the journey is well worth it, as this beach stands among the most renowned in Kanyakumari. This beach serves as the meeting point between the Tamiraparani River and the Arabian Sea, bestowing upon it a unique and captivating allure found nowhere else. To further explore the depths of its beauty, readily accessible catamarans offer an opportunity to delve into the beach's hidden treasures. Additionally, don't miss the chance to visit the neighboring mosque, an ancient structure steeped in history that dates back hundreds of years.

  • Hidden twin beach

    Prepare for a captivating and tranquil beachfront experience that awaits you in Kanyakumari. Indulge in a leisurely and rejuvenating vacation as you immerse yourself in the beauty of Hidden Twin Beach. Delight in the awe-inspiring view of the sunset, strolling barefoot along the shore while the soft sand gently caresses your toes. For a truly enchanting experience, the morning hours offer a serene atmosphere to meditate, as the warm rays of sunlight embrace your face and a refreshing breeze playfully tousles your hair. Conveniently situated near the Bhagavathy Amman Temple, this serene beach is a must-visit during your stay in Kanyakumari.

  • Lemur

    Situated approximately 32 kilometers away from Kanyakumari, Lemur Beach stands as a stunning coastal gem known for its pristine beauty. This exquisite shoreline holds a unique distinction, as it is graced by the waters flowing from the Pechiparai Dam, nourishing numerous fields before gracefully merging with the Arabian Sea. Notably, Lemur Beach has also garnered fame as a favored location for shooting movies, adding to its allure and charm.

Historical places
  • Vattakottai Fort

    Vattakottai Fort, located in Kanyakumari district, is a historical gem that showcases the architectural brilliance of the past. This seaside fortress, constructed during the 18th century by the erstwhile Travancore kingdom, stands as a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the region. Perched on a rocky promontory, Vattakottai Fort offers panoramic views of the mesmerizing Arabian Sea. The fort's name, which translates to "Circular Fort" in the local language, derives from its distinctive circular shape. As you explore the fort, you will be greeted by its impressive stone walls and bastions, providing insights into the defensive strategies employed during its time. Within the fort premises, you can also find a small temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, adding a spiritual touch to the historical ambiance.

  • Jain Temple

    Jain Temple in Chitharal, also known as Thirucharanathupalli, is a revered religious site nestled in the enchanting landscapes of Kanyakumari district. This ancient temple holds immense significance for followers of Jainism and attracts visitors from various parts of the country. The Jain Temple at Chitharal stands as a testament to the rich Jain heritage and architectural grandeur of the past. Believed to have been built during the 9th century, this temple complex showcases exquisite craftsmanship and intricate stone carvings that reflect the artistic skills of the artisans of that era. As you step into the temple premises, you are greeted by the imposing Gajaprishta-style entrance tower, a striking architectural feature. Inside, the temple houses several shrines dedicated to Jain Tirthankaras (spiritual leaders) and deities. The main deity of the temple is Lord Parshvanatha, the 23rd Tirthankara, who is revered by Jains for his teachings on non-violence and spiritual enlightenment.

  • Padmanabhapuram Palace

    Padmanabhapuram Palace, located in the town of Padmanabhapuram in Kanyakumari district, Tamil Nadu, is a magnificent architectural marvel that stands as a testament to the royal grandeur of the erstwhile Travancore Kingdom. Built during the 16th century, this sprawling wooden palace served as the residence of the Travancore rulers for several centuries. The Padmanabhapuram Palace is renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship and intricate detailing. The palace complex showcases a fusion of traditional Kerala and Dravidian architectural styles, reflecting the cultural influences of the region. The intricate wooden carvings, polished floors, ornate ceilings, and beautifully crafted pillars leave visitors awestruck. As you explore the palace, you will encounter various sections that showcase the opulence and elegance of the bygone era. The King's Council Chamber, adorned with rosewood panels and exquisite ceiling artwork, served as a venue for royal meetings and discussions. The Southern Palace, known as Thekke Kottaram, features a stunning four-storey structure with elaborately carved windows and a courtyard adorned with a central pond.

  • Mathur Aqueduct

    The Mathur Aqueduct, situated near the town of Mathur in Kanyakumari district, Tamil Nadu, is an impressive feat of engineering that showcases the ingenuity of its creators. Also known as Mathur Hanging Trough, it stands as one of the longest and highest aqueducts in Asia, spanning across the Pahrali River. Built during the British colonial era in the 19th century, the Mathur Aqueduct was designed to transport water from the Pattanamkal Canal to irrigate the surrounding agricultural lands. The aqueduct is constructed using a combination of brick, stone, and concrete, with a total length of approximately 1,041 feet (316 meters) and a height of about 115 feet (35 meters). The unique aspect of the Mathur Aqueduct lies in its breathtaking structure, where water flows through a series of elevated arches supported by tall pillars. Walking along the aqueduct, visitors are treated to stunning panoramic views of the lush green landscapes, cascading river below, and the surrounding hills.
